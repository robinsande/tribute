Timeline of Steve Jobs Life

  • February 24,1955:

    • February 24, 1955: Steve Jobs is born in San Francisco, California, to Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali. He is adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs. 1972: Jobs graduates from Homestead High School in Cupertino, California

  • 1972 - 1976: Founding Apple

    • 1974: Steve Jobs meets Steve Wozniak, and they begin working together on electronics projects. April 1, 1976: Apple Computer, Inc. is founded by Jobs, Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. They release the Apple I computer.

  • 1977 - 1985: Apple's Early Growth

    • 1977: Apple introduces the Apple II, a highly successful personal computer. 1980: Apple goes public, making Jobs a multimillionaire. 1983: Jobs recruits John Sculley, former PepsiCo executive, to join Apple as CEO. 1984: Apple introduces the Macintosh, featuring the famous "1984" Super Bowl commercial.

  • 1985 - 1996: Departure from Apple and NeXT

    • 1985: After internal conflicts, Steve Jobs is ousted from Apple. He resigns and starts a new company called NeXT, Inc. 1986: Jobs acquires Pixar Animation Studios. 1987: Pixar releases its first short film, "Luxo Jr."

  • 1996 - 1997: Return to Apple

    • 1996: Apple acquires NeXT, bringing Steve Jobs back to the company. 1997: Jobs becomes Apple's interim CEO.

  • 1998 - 2011: Apple's Resurgence and Innovations

    • 1998: Apple introduces the iMac, a successful consumer desktop computer. 2001: Apple unveils the iPod, revolutionizing the music industry. 2003: iTunes Music Store is launched, offering legal music downloads. 2007: Apple releases the iPhone, transforming the smartphone industry. 2010: The iPad is introduced, creating a new market for tablet computers. 2011: Steve Jobs resigns as CEO due to health issues but remains Chairman of the Board.

  • 2011 - 2019: Passing and Legacy

    • October 5, 2011: Steve Jobs passes away at the age of 56 due to complications from pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor. His biography, "Steve Jobs" by Walter Isaacson, is published posthumously. Apple continues to thrive under the leadership of Tim Cook, with products like the iPhone, iPad, and Macintosh. Steve Jobs' contributions to technology, design, and innovation left an indelible mark on the world, and his legacy continues to influence the tech industry and inspire entrepreneurs to this day.
